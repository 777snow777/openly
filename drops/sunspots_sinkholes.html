sunspots,sinkholes

my dreams feel like
patches of dirt
outside the church
the metal bench
next to the river
playing backwards
picture by picture
letter by letter

my dreams come like sunspots
blood clots
my dreams are speckled pink
sunbeams
lists of things you love
mini skirts and speaking matter of factly
my words are a loveless breed torn between
my clumsy low pitch and your mumbly accent
in fact
my voice is a remnant of everyone i've ever loved
and i hope i never stop sounding like you
never stop speaking in long, clear sentences
speaking til my words drip and crumble and fray at the ends
thats how you sound with my chin on your stomach
thats how you sound when you talk about home
when you have so much to say but dont
my dreams are speckled pink, menial things that actually happened
you walked me to the bus stop
you invited me to your grandmother's house
then avoided me for 3 months
you prayed for me and I didn't understand why
i laid in the grass field outside
the church on Sunday morning
i'd happily make Sunday mornings my eternity
spent counting people on their balconies
the sun pouring into my eyes
waiting for you
