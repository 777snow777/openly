still my love

the hum of pop songs twisting through now empty streets
hanging between car windows and bird whistles
my love is through the wind, caught in your teeth
fermented with months i spend longing
find it years later buried
in the weeds, cracking through the concrete
next to the roots of the tall trees
marked with a letter in bold black ink
painfully obvious, but more painfully protected
my love is the water for my reflection
rippling with the times i have forgiven
but it is so still and clear in your presence
